About Me

Hello, my name is Matthew I am a graduating senior at Cornell University and PhD applicant at MIT Media Lab. My background is in Human Computer Interaction (HCI), Design Methods, and UX/UI. I have worked on projects using varying approaches to the Human Centered Design process including diary studies, surveys, interviews, and mixed method studies. My research interest is in Augmented Reality(AR) and using this tool to enhance cognitive capabilities (learning, memory, attention, language), promote self-expression, and fulfill socio-emotional needs. Through research my goal is to address questions like these:

  • What body gestures/motions are neccessary for optimal usability in AR applications?
  • How can AR be used to support positive self-expressive experiences?
  • What are the applications of AR in improving coginitive processes?
  • How are socio-emotional applications of AR perceived in humans?

Currently, I am a member of the Cornell Social Media Lab. We are working on a research project in conjunction with the Weil Medical Center to provide refugees living in New York City with accessible information management tools during the pandemic.
